Congratulations on your Webby Award! How does it feel to be recognized at this level?
Thank you! It's incredibly humbling. At Maitra Web Tech, we've always believed in pushing boundaries, but to have that recognized by the Webbys—an institution that celebrates internet excellence—is surreal. The award validates our approach to design as an emotional experience rather than just visual aesthetics.
Your winning project stood out for its "cinematic web experience." Can you explain that concept?
Traditional websites are static—you scroll, you click. We asked: what if navigating a website felt like moving through a beautifully directed film? We applied principles from cinematography—lighting, pacing, scene transitions—to web interactions. For example, scrolling triggers camera-like movements through 3D space, and hover states reveal narrative elements. It's web design as storytelling medium.

What technical innovations made this possible?
Three key technologies: First, Three.js for GPU-accelerated 3D that works across devices. Second, GSAP for buttery smooth animations synchronized to scroll. Third, a custom "motion choreography" system that orchestrates hundreds of micro-interactions into a cohesive flow. The real magic was making this complexity feel effortless to the user.
"Parnava's work represents the next evolution of web design—where technology serves emotion rather than dominates it. The jury was unanimous in their decision." — Webby Awards Jury Statement
How does your creative process differ from traditional web design?
We start with mood reels instead of mood boards—short films that capture the emotional journey. Our wireframes include motion notes. And we prototype early with real content because timing affects emotional impact. At Maitra Web Tech, our designers storyboard interactions like film directors, considering pacing and dramatic tension.
What advice would you give to designers aspiring to this level?
Master the fundamentals, then forget them. Learn cinema. Study how Spielberg directs attention or how Villeneuve builds atmosphere. Then translate those techniques to the web. Tools matter less than vision—our award-winning project used the same basic tech stack as any site, just with more intention behind every decision.
What's next for Maitra Web Tech?
We're developing "Maitra Jarvis"—an AI interface that adapts not just content but entire interaction models to individual users. Imagine a website that reorganizes its navigation based on your behavior patterns. It's risky, it's ambitious, and it might just win us another Webby!
